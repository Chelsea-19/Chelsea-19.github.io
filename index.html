Selected Projects

Representative work across genomic modeling, medical agent systems, and applied AI workflows. Each project reflects a shared theme: turning advanced models into structured, trustworthy, and decision-oriented systems.

NTU Research Project · Genomics · Foundation Models

Evo 2 Genomic Language Model

Designed and implemented a multi-scale perplexity analysis framework for Acinetobacter baumannii, spanning replicon-level, fixed-window, and gene-centric dynamic-window analysis to identify regions deviating from species-level statistical grammar.

  • Built the experimental pipeline from hypothesis framing to interpretation and visualization
  • Developed scripts for long-sequence inference, perplexity analysis, and embedding extraction under HPC constraints
  • Extended the research direction toward a lab-embedded Genomic Agent architecture with distillation and retrieval grounding
Parexel · Clinical AI · RWD/RWE Workflow

Patient Recruitment Agent (PRA)

Contributed to workflow abstraction and task decomposition for an AI-assisted patient recruitment system in clinical research, with emphasis on eligibility logic, structured extraction, and deployment realism in CRO settings.

  • Mapped clinical text into structured eligibility conditions and downstream storage logic
  • Examined how agent systems fit regulatory, operational, and business constraints
  • Built a practical understanding of AI adoption across APAC pharmaceutical and clinical research workflows
NTU Research Internship · Medical Education Agent

SimuPatient

Reframed an LLM-based standardized patient prototype into a more research-ready educational agent system, covering case schema design, dialogue control, assessment logic, and structured logging.

  • Redesigned the pipeline from case generation to consultation, logging, and scoring
  • Introduced persona consistency, hidden-case logic, and LLM-as-a-Judge style evaluation
  • Helped define the path from demo prototype to a reproducible research artifact
Biomedical AI Agent · Drug Repurposing

OrphanCure

Developed a verifiable AI agent prototype for rare-disease drug repurposing by chaining entity resolution, target retrieval, literature search, model reasoning, and evidence checking into a staged workflow.

  • Designed stage-based agent states with human-in-the-loop entity resolution
  • Integrated OpenTargets and PubMed into a retrieval-augmented biomedical analysis flow
  • Reduced hallucination risk through schema-constrained outputs and post-hoc verification

Research Interests

I am motivated by problems where frontier AI models need to operate under real scientific, clinical, or operational constraints.

Topics

  • Genomic language models and zero-shot biological signal discovery
  • Medical and biomedical AI agents with state, tools, and safety layers
  • Retrieval, verification, and evidence-backed reasoning systems
  • AI productization in healthcare, life sciences, and scientific workflows

Working Style

  • Start from the scientific or operational problem, not from a model trend alone
  • Emphasize workflow design, structured outputs, logging, and human-in-the-loop control
  • Care about the path from demo to research artifact, then to product prototype
  • Prefer systems that are verifiable, deployable, and useful in real environments
