NTU Research Project · Genomics · Foundation Models · AI for Science

Evo 2 Genomic Language Model

I designed and implemented a multi-scale perplexity analysis framework for Acinetobacter baumannii, spanning replicon-level, fixed-window, and gene-centric dynamic-window analysis. The central question was whether language-model signals could be used not only for prediction, but also for interpreting biological regularities and identifying regions that deviate from species-level genomic grammar.

  • Built the experimental pipeline from hypothesis framing to interpretation and visualization
  • Developed scripts for long-sequence inference, perplexity analysis, and embedding extraction under HPC constraints
  • Extended the research direction toward a lab-embedded Genomic Agent architecture with distillation and retrieval grounding

Parexel · Clinical AI · RWD/RWE Workflow

Patient Recruitment Agent (PRA)

In this work, I contributed to the workflow design of an AI-assisted patient recruitment system for clinical research. Although clinically oriented, the project also shaped my research perspective on scientific AI systems: good performance depends not only on extraction quality, but on how reasoning steps, constraints, and evidence are organized across the full workflow.

  • Mapped clinical text into structured eligibility conditions and downstream storage logic
  • Examined how agent systems fit regulatory, operational, and business constraints
  • Built a practical understanding of AI adoption across APAC pharmaceutical and clinical research workflows
NTU Research Internship · Medical Education Agent

SimuPatient

I helped rethink an LLM-based standardized patient prototype as a more research-ready educational agent system. This meant moving beyond a functional demo and asking how the system could support reproducibility, structured evaluation, and more rigorous study in medical education settings.

  • Redesigned the pipeline from case generation to consultation, logging, and scoring
  • Introduced persona consistency, hidden-case logic, and LLM-as-a-Judge style evaluation
  • Helped define the path from demo prototype to a reproducible research artifact
Biomedical AI Agent · Drug Repurposing

OrphanCure

I developed a verifiable AI agent prototype for rare-disease drug repurposing by connecting entity resolution, target retrieval, literature search, model reasoning, and evidence checking into one staged workflow. For me, this project reflected a core AI for Science idea: an answer is more useful when it is connected to traceable evidence, explicit reasoning stages, and domain-relevant verification.

  • Designed stage-based agent states with human-in-the-loop entity resolution
  • Integrated OpenTargets and PubMed into a retrieval-augmented biomedical analysis flow
  • Reduced hallucination risk through schema-constrained outputs and post-hoc verification

Research Interests

I am most motivated by problems where AI models need to operate under real scientific, biological, or clinical constraints, and where interpretation matters as much as performance.

Topics

  • Genomic language models and zero-shot biological signal discovery
  • Medical and biomedical AI agents with state, tools, and safety layers
  • Retrieval, verification, and evidence-backed reasoning systems
  • AI for Science workflows in healthcare, life sciences, and computational biology

Working Style

  • Start from the scientific question or biological problem, not from a model trend alone
  • Emphasize workflow design, structured outputs, logging, and human-in-the-loop control
  • Care about the path from exploratory prototype to research artifact and robust scientific workflow
  • Prefer systems that are verifiable, deployable, and useful in real environments
