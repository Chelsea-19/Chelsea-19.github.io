Bio · Foundation Models · NTU Research

Evo 2 Genomic Language Model

Designed a multi-scale interpretation framework for Acinetobacter baumannii, spanning replicon-level, fixed-window, dynamic-window, and embedding-based analyses to study species-level genomic regularities and anomalous regions.

  • Built analysis pipelines for long-sequence inference, gene-centric perplexity, and representation extraction.
  • Connected exploratory analysis to a broader genomic agent direction with retrieval and distillation ideas.
  • Positioned the work as an interpretable AI4Science study rather than a benchmark-only exercise.
Why it matters: demonstrates how foundation-model signals can support biological interpretation, not only prediction.
